Birth in Anenecuilco, Morelos

Zapata was born to Gabriel Zapata, a mestizo peasant who trained and sold horses, and Cleofas Jertrudiz Salazar. He eventually had three brothers, Eufemio, Pedro, and Loreto, and three sisters, Matilde, Jovita, Maria de Jesus, Celsa, Maria del la Luz and Romana.

Map of Morelos

Orphaned at age 16

Zapata’s father passed away at age 16. His mother died 11 months later, leaving him to take care of his many siblings.

Hacienda Santa Inés en Cuautla, Morelos

Arrested for protesting

At age 18, Zapata was arrested for taking part in peasant protest against a hacienda which had appropriated their lands.

Drafted into Mexican Army

After obtaining a pardon, Zapata was drafted into the Mexican army, but served only six months. He was discharged to a landowner in Mexico City to train horses, a skill he had gained from his father.
