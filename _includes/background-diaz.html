stolen land

The Mexican social and economic system was dominated by large estates (haciendas) controlling much of the land and squeezing the holdings of independent communities. Many peasants were subsequently forced into debt peonage (peonaje) on the haciendas.

Peasants were forced into taking jobs with long hours and poor working conditions to pay off debt. Peasants had few rights, most of which were neglected.
